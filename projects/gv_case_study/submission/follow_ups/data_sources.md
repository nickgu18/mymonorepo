# What other data sources would you want to integrate to improve your scoring?

To build a more holistic view of a founder's potential, I would integrate several external data sources to augment the existing dataset.

0. Personality Traits (derived)

Analyze social media presence, blogs, and public speaking engagements to categorize founders using Merrill-Reid Social Personality Styles (Driver, Analytical, Expressive, Amiable). This can help identify founders with the leadership and communication styles most correlated with success in different industries.

1. Social Networks

Committees, boards, and expanded professional connections beyond immediate co-workers. This provides a broader view of a founder's influence and access to resources.

2. Social Signal

LinkedIn activity, Twitter/X engagement, and GitHub contributions for technical founders.
This provides real-time insights into a founder's thought leadership, community engagement, and technical proficiency. For technical founders, GitHub activity (stars, contributions) is a strong proxy for skill and influence.

3. Financial Data

Crunchbase, Pitchbook, or Dealroom.
While we have some performance data, these platforms offer more accurate and comprehensive records of funding rounds, valuations, and investor quality. This would allow for better validation of previous success and more precise label generation.

4. Patent Data

USPTO or Google Patents.
For deep tech and life sciences, patent filings are a primary indicator of intellectual property value and technical defensibility. Founders with a strong patent portfolio often have a higher probability of successful exits in these sectors.

5. News & Media

News APIs, TechCrunch, and general media outlets.
Sentiment analysis on news articles mentioning the founder or their previous companies can reveal public perception, leadership style, and potential red flags not captured in structured data.

6. Academic Citations

Google Scholar or Semantic Scholar.
For founders emerging from academia, citation counts and publication impact (h-index) are crucial metrics for assessing their expertise and the potential impact of their research-based startups.
