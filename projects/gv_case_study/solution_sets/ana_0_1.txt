This document gives further explanation to select columns in the datasets given to you.


founder_experience


* person_id - A UUID for every person.
* order - The chronological order of each experience, per person.
* company_id - A UUID for every company. [prev missing]
* title - The job title held during this experience. [prev missing]
* job_type - The category or type of job (e.g., full-time, part-time, advisory). [prev missing]
* start_date - The date this experience began. [prev missing]
* end_date - Current roles will have a null end_date. A null end date however does not necessarily indicate a current role. It could just mean info is missing.
* is_c_suite - If True, the person held a C-suite (executive) role. [prev missing]
* is_employee - If True, the person was an employee of the company. [prev missing]
* is_executive - If True, the person held an executive-level position. [prev missing]
* is_founder - If True, it means the person is a founder of a company. [prev missing]
* is_on_board - If True, it means the person is on the board of directors of a company.
person_id,order,company_id,title,job_type,start_date,end_date,is_c_suite,is_employee,is_executive,is_founder,is_on_board



education_training


* person_id - The same UUID that identifies people in founder_experience.
* education_tier - A measure of the quality of a person’s education (independent of the highest_level -value), scored discretely from 1-5.
* highest_level - A string of highest degree level [prev missing]


company_info
* company_id - A UUID for every company.
* industry - A industry of the company [prev missing]
* performance - A measure of the level of achievement for companies. Whereas multiple in target_variable_training.csv requires a company to have exited, this ‘performance’ measure applies to companies that have not exited as well. It is a related measure, but the exact details of the computation are not necessary to know. Just know a larger number indicates a better performing company. Many companies will have a Null for this field.


target_variable_training
* company_id - A UUID for every company, the same as in company_info.
* company_founded  - The year in which that company was founded.
* multiple - The return on capital venture capitalists achieved by investing in an early financing round of this company, expressed as a ratio of ($ returned / $ invested). A multiple of 1.0 for instance would indicate no profit, and no loss of money. This is the core metric that VCs want to optimize for.
